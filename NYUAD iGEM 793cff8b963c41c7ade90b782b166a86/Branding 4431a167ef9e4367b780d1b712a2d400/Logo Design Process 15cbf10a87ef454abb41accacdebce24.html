Logo Design Process

First Iteration

  • Simple drawing of the falcon is meant to represent the NYUAD falcon (Note: Look at branding guidelines for use of the falcon)
  • No use of reference photos
  • Wanted to use a circular format because it can be manipulated to fit many other uses (i.e. pins, stickers, embroidered patches for clothing, facebook profile photos, etc.)
  • Overwhelming amount of purple, lack of contrast between the white and purple
  • Lack elements leads to imbalance of the logo
  • If falcon were refined and the text were evenly spaced this might be a plausible variant, but I don't see it as being a prospect for the main logo
  • Lack of emphasis
  • All elements look equally important
  • Imbalance

Second Iteration

  • After doing some research and looking at how past iGEM teams manipulated the iGEM logo to show their school's personality I tried to manipulate the iGEM logo's original design and replace the amoeba with the an illustration the desert falcon, our school's mascot. I replaced the falcon's eye with a gear to replicate the original design of the amoeba. In addition, I added the four flamed torch element on top of the 'i' to refer to the NYUAD torch.
  • After adding these elements to the base design, my first dilemma was between whether to place the name NYUAD above or below. After seeing both versions, I decided that the having the NYUAD name above balanced the logo better overall.
  • In addition, I also experimented with other colors. I used 2 versions of green that are both used in the original iGEM logo but after seeing the results, I decided that NYUAD purple seemed to be the most fitting color to use color for the logo
  • However, some issues of this logo include:
    • Imbalanced kerning between the letters 'i' and 'e' with 'g' leading to visual discomfort
    • Lack of spacing within the falcon from the size of the 'g'
  • These issues led this iteration of the logo to look unappealing overall
