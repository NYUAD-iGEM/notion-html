Equipment Required

"The minimum equipment required to run the protocol following RNA extraction includes Eppendorf tubes with reagents, heat blocks or water bath (37 °C and 62 °C), nuclease-free water, pipettes and tips and lateral flow strips." However, RNA extraction itself requires multiple steps, centrifugation, and the materials provided in the RNA Extraction kit with a particular procedure with set liquid quantities and centrifugation steps. Use of LFA

The CRISPR process, if activated in presence of the target RNA, cleaves a reporter construct (made of Biotin and FITC/FAM) which creates a readout on applying the solution to the pre-made LFA strip specific to the reporter.

Key Findings

Even though the method uses the universal LFA strip (cost ~3$) which is easy to readout, dispose, and store, the pre-processing required for the RNA Extraction which involves specific centrifugation and precise steps makes it difficult for a POC diagnostic device. However, it should be kept into mind that this RNA extraction was for a viral RNA which could significantly differ in case of Bd/Bsal fungus.
